Testing & Treatment

Knowledge is power. Early testing and treatment can save lives and protect your community.

Importance of Testing

Getting tested for HIV is the first and most crucial step towards taking control of your health.

Where to Get Tested

  • Local clinics and hospitals
  • Community health centers
  • Mobile testing units
  • Specialized HIV testing centers
  • Pharmacies offering rapid HIV tests

When to Get Tested

  • After unprotected sex
  • After sharing needles or blood exposure
  • During pregnancy
  • Regularly if at higher risk
  • Immediately after possible exposure

How Testing Works

  • Rapid tests use blood or saliva, results in 20 minutes
  • Lab tests are more detailed but slower
  • Testing is confidential and often free or low-cost
Understanding ART (Antiretroviral Therapy)

Benefits of ART

  • Reduces viral load to undetectable levels
  • Supports the immune system
  • Prevents transmission (U=U)
  • Improves quality of life

Importance of Adherence

  • Take medication exactly as prescribed
  • Missed doses can lead to drug resistance
  • Follow-up with healthcare providers
  • Use support groups or counseling if needed
