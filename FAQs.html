Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between HIV and AIDS?

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) attacks the immune system. AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome) is the most advanced stage of HIV infection.

How is HIV transmitted?

HIV is transmitted via blood, semen, vaginal fluids, rectal fluids, and breast milk. It cannot be spread through casual contact.

Can HIV be prevented?

Yes. Safe sex, not sharing needles, PrEP, and screening blood products can prevent HIV.

Is there a cure for HIV?

There is currently no cure, but ART allows people with HIV to live healthy lives.

Why is regular HIV testing important?

Regular testing helps with early diagnosis, timely treatment, and reduces the risk of transmission.
