About HIV & AIDS

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that attacks the body's immune system. If left untreated, it can lead to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome).

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that attacks the body's immune system, specifically the CD4 cells (T cells), which help the immune system fight off infections. If left untreated, HIV reduces the number of CD4 cells in the body, making the person more likely to get infections or infection-related cancers.

AIDS is the late stage of HIV infection that occurs when the body's immune system is badly damaged because of the virus.HIV targets the body's CD4 cells (T cells), which help the immune system fight off infections. Without treatment, HIV reduces the number of CD4 cells, making the body more vulnerable to infections and diseases.

Over time, HIV can destroy so many of these cells that the body can't fight off infections and disease. These opportunistic infections or cancers take advantage of a very weak immune system and signal that the person has AIDS, the last stage of HIV infection. No effective cure currently exists for HIV. But with proper medical care, HIV can be controlled. People with HIV who get effective HIV treatment can live long, healthy lives and protect their partners.
