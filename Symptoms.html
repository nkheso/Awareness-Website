HIV Symptoms

Early Symptoms (2-4 weeks after infection)

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Rash
  • Night sweats
  • Muscle aches
  • Sore throat
  • Fatigue
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Mouth ulcers

Clinical Latency Stage

  • May not have any symptoms
  • HIV is still active but reproduces at low levels
  • Without treatment, this period can last a decade or longer
  • Some people progress through this stage faster
  • Can still transmit HIV to others

Symptoms of AIDS

  • Rapid weight loss
  • Recurring fever or profuse night sweats
  • Extreme and unexplained tiredness
  • Prolonged swelling of lymph glands
  • Diarrhea that lasts for more than a week
  • Sores of the mouth, anus, or genitals
  • Pneumonia
  • Memory loss, depression, other neurologic disorders

Important Note

Many of these symptoms can be caused by other illnesses. The only way to know if you have HIV is to get tested. If you think you may have been exposed to HIV, talk to a healthcare provider about testing options.
